## Idea
ASTRA — AI-driven Scam Threat Recognition Assistant
Real-time, explainable scam detection across calls & SMS — in Telugu, Tenglish, Hindi & English
Problem: India lost ₹1,087 crore to UPI fraud in FY24 alone (13.42 lakh incidents), and digital financial fraud crossed ₹4,245 crore across 2.4 million incidents in just the first 10 months of FY25. A nationwide survey found 1 in 5 UPI households have faced fraud, 51% never report it, and even when reported, only about 6% of chargeback claims are successfully recovered. Scams hit through both calls and SMS, yet tools like Truecaller work on caller-ID reputation, not on understanding the actual content — and none handle regional, code-switched speech like Tenglish or Hinglish.
The App: ASTRA is a simple two-button app — Record Call and Scan Message. For a suspicious call, the user puts it on speakerphone and taps record; for a suspicious SMS or WhatsApp text, they forward it straight into the app via the share sheet. Within seconds, ASTRA transcribes the audio (or reads the text) and runs it through a multilingual scam classifier trained on real Indian fraud patterns — OTP theft, UPI scams, fake loans, banking impersonation. The result screen shows a clear risk score, highlights the exact words that triggered it, and gives a plain-language next step: block the number, don't share the OTP, report to the 1930 cybercrime helpline. The interface itself is available in Telugu and Hindi, not just the backend detection, so it's usable by elderly and non-English-fluent users — the group most targeted and least protected today.

Honest limitation: True live call-audio interception needs default-dialer-level access, restricted on Android/iOS by design — our MVP uses speakerphone recording instead, which also matches real behavior since suspicion usually kicks in mid-call, not from second one.

Impact: One unified app covering both major scam channels, explainable results that build lasting scam literacy instead of just blocking a number, and full support for the languages and channels people are actually scammed in.
